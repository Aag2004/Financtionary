A

Amortization
The spreading out of capital expenses for intangible assets over a specific duration.
Angel Investor
An affluent individual who provides capital for a business start-up, usually in exchange for convertible debt or ownership equity.
Annuity
A financial product that pays out a fixed stream of payments to an individual, primarily used as an income stream for retirees.
Arbitrage
The simultaneous purchase and sale of an asset to profit from an imbalance in the price.
Asset
Anything owned by an individual or entity that has economic value.
Audit
An official inspection of an individual's or organization's accounts, typically by an independent body.

B

Balance Sheet
A financial statement that reports a company's assets, liabilities, and shareholders' equity at a specific point in time.
Bear Market
A market condition where prices are falling or expected to fall.
Blue Chip Stocks
Shares in large, well-known companies with a solid history of financial performance.
Bond
A fixed-income instrument representing a loan made by an investor to a borrower.
Budget
An estimate of income and expenditure for a set period.
Bull Market
A market condition where prices are rising or expected to rise.

C

Capital
Financial assets or the financial value of assets, such as funds held in deposit accounts.
Capital Gain
The profit from the sale of an asset or investment.
Cash Flow Statement
A financial statement that shows how changes in balance sheet accounts and income affect cash and cash equivalents.
Credit
An agreement where a borrower receives something of value now and agrees to repay the lender at a later date.
Credit Default Swap (CDS)
A financial derivative that allows an investor to swap or offset their credit risk with that of another investor.
Credit Rating
An assessment of the creditworthiness of a borrower in general terms or with respect to a particular debt or financial obligation.
Credit Score
A numerical expression based on a level analysis of a person's credit files to represent the creditworthiness of an individual.
Custodian
A financial institution that holds customers' securities for safekeeping to prevent them from being stolen or lost.

D

Debit
An accounting entry that results in either an increase in assets or a decrease in liabilities on a company's balance sheet.
Deflation
The reduction of the general level of prices in an economy.
Depreciation
The reduction in the value of an asset over time, particularly in regards to tangible assets.
Derivatives
Financial contracts whose value is derived from the value of an underlying asset.
Diversification
The strategy of spreading investments across various financial instruments to reduce risk.
Dividend
A portion of a company's earnings distributed to shareholders.

E

Emerging Markets
Nations with social or business activities in the process of rapid growth and industrialization.
Endowment
A financial asset, in the form of a donation made to a non-profit group or institution.
Equity
The ownership interest in an asset or business, calculated as the difference between assets and liabilities.
Exchange-Traded Fund (ETF)
A type of investment fund traded on stock exchanges, much like stocks.
Expense
The economic costs a business incurs through its operations to earn revenue.

F

Futures
Financial contracts obligating the buyer to purchase an asset or the seller to sell an asset at a predetermined future date and price.
Financial Engineering
The use of mathematical techniques to solve financial problems or create financial products.
Financial Statement
A formal record of the financial activities and position of a business, person, or entity.
Fiscal Policy
Government policies regarding taxation and spending to influence the economy.

G

Gross Domestic Product (GDP)
The total value of all goods and services produced in a country over a specific time period.
